## The middle region needs a real job

If a visual module can disappear without changing the page, it is probably decorative. A strong homepage should not rely on a pile of small widgets. It should have a clear visual center of gravity.

This visual area does three things:

- gives the page a memorable graphic identity
- creates breathing room between the information-heavy side columns
- adds a controlled sense of system activity

## Why this version stays CSS-first

The center visual uses gradients, rings, points, and restrained motion instead of a heavy canvas layer.

That choice keeps two things under control:

- runtime cost
- content hierarchy

## Motion should support rhythm

This page only needs three kinds of motion:

- staged boot-in on load
- gentle drift and pulse in the visual nucleus
- hover feedback on rows and links

Anything beyond that starts to make the page louder than it needs to be.
