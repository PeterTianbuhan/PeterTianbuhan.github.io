## The start point was not blank

The real time-saver was not inventing a homepage from scratch. It was keeping the parts of the draft that already had some conviction: mood, hierarchy, and page balance.

Three decisions stayed in place:

- the left column carries identity, skills, and projects
- the center remains the visual nucleus
- the right column handles profile state, code, and writing

## Why content had to move out of components

If copy lives inside layout code, every content edit becomes a design edit. That is the wrong maintenance model for a personal site, because the content will change far more often than the composition.

So the refactor splits the site into two layers:

1. layout and visual components
2. replaceable structured data and MDX content

That gives the homepage room to evolve without forcing a page rewrite every time the owner updates their profile.

## Why local MDX fits this site

For a personal site, reliability and control usually matter more than a full CMS.

Local MDX keeps the workflow simple:

- content and code live in the same repository
- bilingual routing is easy to reason about
- article metadata, code blocks, and navigation can grow naturally over time

## What matters next for me

The next high-value step is not adding more effects. It is replacing placeholders with more believable material:

- my real name, year, and short introduction
- course projects, side experiments, or competition work
- a few posts that reflect how I think at this stage

That is when the site stops feeling like a polished shell and starts reading as a credible personal presence.
